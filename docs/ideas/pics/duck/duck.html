The Golden Egg



The story of Little Duck for my daughter.




Little Duck, Little Duck where do you go? Searching here, searching there, looking high, looking low? "I'm off to find an Easter Egg," says Duck, all bright and bold. "A very special egg indeed - an egg that's made of GOLD!"


Little Duck, Little Duck what have you found? Looking here, looking there, searching all around? "I've found a lovely shiny egg. I've found an egg, it's true. But this egg isn't gold at all..." "Oh no." says Chick "It's BLUE!"


"But what's that glittering over there?" says Chick all eagerly. So side-by-side they go and look. Whatever can it be? And there amongst the garden flowers they find to their surprise ... A shiny egg of ruby RED all ringed with butterflies.


They search the sunny meadow. The lambs leap as they pass. And there they find another egg, that's lying in the grass. It glitters in the sunshine. Chick chirps a load "Hurray!" But Duck just says ... "It's PURPLE," and carries on her way.


They stop beside a babbling brook, and Duck lets out a "Quack!" Swan is swimming down the stream and, cradled on her back, there is an egg so dazzling, so bright it makes them blink. But is it gold? "Oh no," says Swan ... "My egg is rosy PINK!"


Into the wood goes Little Duck to summon further help. The woodland creatures scurry up and one lets out a yelp! "I've found an egg," says Fox Cub. "The best I've ever seen ..." And nestled in the dappled shade lies Foxy's egg of GREEN.


Just then two rabbits scamper up, and then another four, "We are the Easter Bunnies and we've Easter Eggs galore. Come look inside our basket - there's surely one for you ..." But all their eggs are SILVER and simply will not do.


Says Little Duck "These Easter Eggs are beautiful indeed. But one made out of solid gold is what I chiefly need." "Then you must find the Golden Goose," Duck hears a bunny say. "And if you'd like to follow me, I'll gladly lead the way.


So off the set and soon they find a Goose upon her nest. She looks a little puzzled when she hears of Duck's request. "I do not have a egg of gold," she says and shakes her head. "But here's a lovely Easter Egg that you can have instead. ...


It's gold and red and pink and blue, green and silver and purple too. ... But underneath the wrapping it's just like all the rest ... For everyone knows Easter Eggs of CHOCOLATE are the best!"
