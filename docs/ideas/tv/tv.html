Should Vampirates Be A Film Or TV Series?



At the Windsor Festival Justin Somper hinted that he had been approached about Vampirates being an animated TV series.



On The Blue Peter Blog Justin asked:
'Vampirates' into a movie. I want to hear your views on books vs movies. Do you enjoy seeing your fave books like Stormbreaker, Harry Potter and Narnia being made into movies? Do the movies live up to the books? Are the movies a good representation of the books for fans coming to the story fresh? Does a not-so-great movie put you off going back to the books? Your thoughts on this subject please!

So what are your views? please send via the contact (squeak) page.

Comment and question from The Nocturn on the Blue Peter Blog
"I like the idea of seeing an amazing book being turned into a film. Sometimes after reading a brill book, you can imagine them making good movies. I think Vampirates would make awesome movies. But I do try not to get to hyped up because as the movie adaptation of Eragon by Christopher Paolini proved a massive disappointment I don't want to see a brilliant book be butchered. I like when they are true to the books. Rather like the Harry Potter films although some things are added and changed, they are roughly accurate.
Which of the three Vampirates books you have written do you feel, personally, would make the best movie? Out of the two we can read, I think Tide of Terror as it comes on leaps and bounds from Demons of the Ocean."
Answer "Which of my books would make the best movie? I think that because of the continuing story arc, a movie team would probably want to take the first three or four books together rather than just adapting one book. I think that would deliver a more satisfying story. That said, I think it would be really interesting to develop a fresh story for a movie, which brought new and existing fans into the lives of the characters from a different angle."
