The Recruiting Officer


By Justin Somper



Desperado’s the name. Johnny Desperado. Captain Johnny Desperado, to give me my full title. Yes, I thought that might get your attention! Even in the midst of war, the rank of Captain is one to summon instant respect – especially a Vampirate Captain. I guess my generously sized canine teeth aren’t unhelpful in the respect stakes either. Know what I mean? Matter of fact, I’m just heading out on an important mission. I’m recruiting for my bosses, Sidorio and Lady Lola. That’s right – Sid and Lola. Believe everything you’ve ever heard about them. They are utterly ruthless, evil incarnate but also, as it happens, a lot of fun when you get to know them.

See, I have a rendezvous with a land-based vampire clan. No names, no latitude or longitude – these guys wouldn’t thank me for disclosing their digits. I’m already sharing more than I should, but you’re really easy to talk to. All you need to know is that these dudes are majorly powerful and, like me, they dig their blood. But their power is limited because they’re old-school – they live on the land whereas everyone knows real power resides in those of us who rule the oceans.

I’m heading out to meet this vampire chieftain to make him an offer. We’re always looking to join up with others and bring them into our force. We’re really inclusive! I’m travelling with a few gifts, courtesy of Sid and Lola – some priceless golden goblets, which once belonged to some dame by the name of Cleopatra and a few bottles of vintage blood. That should help get the conversation off on the right footing. But really, when it comes down to it, all anyone is interested in is power. Wouldn’t you agree? And I can offer power in abundance. Just as soon as this guy signs his autograph on the dotted line.

So that’s the plan for later but I’m running ahead of schedule, so I think I’ll stop off for a quick drink. My first choice would be a Blood Tavern but there isn’t one in this neck of the woods – no pun intended – so I’ll make do with this pirate tavern. What’s it called? The Full Moon Saloon. I like it already. Yeah, I reckon I’ll stop in on the opposition, see if I can pick up any useful gossip. Follow me inside if you like, but keep your distance. No offence and all, my friend but, as the saying goes, he travels the fastest who travels alone!

Hey, I thought I told you to keep your distance! Though now I see you in this light, I have to say, I had no idea how pleasing to the glance you are. Those green eyes, that long dark hair - the kind that leads people more poetic than me to make comparisons to ravens’ wings. Not that you see too many ravens in these parts, eh?

And you’ve bought me a drink? You really shouldn’t have. No, you really shouldn’t have. I’m not much interested in the drinks they offer in these places – pirate drinks. I’d much rather drink a pirate. Know what I mean? Yeah, you’re quick on the uptake, missy.

So, why don’t you tell me about yourself? Feels like I’m the one who’s been doing all the talking here. Cat got your tongue? What does that expression even mean? All right then, I’ll take a sip of this drink. Rude not to. And no-one has ever accused Johnny Desperado of rudeness - not before, nor since, I made the rank of captain.

Man, this stuff packs a punch. Whew! What did they put in this? Aconite, you say. Whoah, sugar, don’t you know that aconite is a very, very unfriendly substance to Vampirates? Oh, I get it. You did know that.

Hey, how come there’s two of you now? And when exactly did the room start spinning around like a fairground carousel? Think I might need some fresh air. Yes, that’s what ol’ Johnny needs. Some fresh air. Could you maybe give me a hand, sugar? I’m not feeling too steady on my feet all of a sudden.

So now you’re backing away from me? You’re not going to help, ol’ Johnny, are you? Neither of you is going to help me! Oh, I know there’s just one of you. It’s the aconite playing tricks with my mind.

What’s that you say? Just enough to slow me down and take me out of action for a few hours? We’ll see about that. I got me a rendezvous to get to. Like I told you before. That vampire chieftain is expecting me.

Why are you smiling, sugar? Because I ain’t going nowhere tonight? Says who? I got an appointment to keep. I got me a war to win. I guess we’re on opposite sides then. Are you some kind of pirate officer? I asked you a question. Hey, where you going? Come back here. Come back…
